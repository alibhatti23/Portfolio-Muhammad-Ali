Running a Shopify store often means you need help. Whether you’re hiring a developer for custom theme work, a contractor to add products, or a team member to manage orders you need a safe way to grant access.

Here’s the problem: Shopify’s basic plan limits you to just 2 staff members. Growth plan? 5 staff members. Advanced? 15. But what if you need more?

The solution is Shopify collaborator codes. This method lets you invite unlimited users without hitting plan limitations and maintain complete control over what they can access.

---

## Why Collaborator Codes Are Better Than Staff Accounts

Before diving into the steps, understand why collaborator codes are the smart choice:

**Advantages:**

- No plan limitations (works on all Shopify plans)
- Granular permission control
- Easy to revoke access instantly
- Safer for temporary contractors
- Doesn’t count toward staff account limits
- Developers can request access without you creating accounts

---

## Step 1: Generate Your Collaborator Code

    1. Log in to your Shopify store admin dashboard
    2. Click the Settings icon (gear icon in the bottom left corner)
    3. Select “Users and permissions” from the menu
    4. Click on the “Security” tab
    5. Look for “Invite third-party collaborators”
    6. Copy your collaborator code
    7. Your collaborator code is unique to your store and never changes. You can share it with multiple people.

---

## Step 2: Get Your Store URL

Along with your collaborator code, you’ll need your store URL in this format:

yourstore.myshopify.com

This appears right next to your collaborator code in the Security section.

---

## Step 3: Share With Your Developer or Collaborator

What to send them:

- Your collaborator code
- Your store URL
Share these only with people you trust using secure communication like email or Slack.

---

## Step 4: Developer Requests Access

Once you’ve shared the code and URL, here’s what your developer should do:

**For Developers Using Shopify Dev Dashboard:**

    1. Go to the Shopify Dev Dashboard
    2. Click “Request access”
    3. Paste your store URL
    4. Enter the collaborator code
    5. Select specific permissions (theme development, online store, products, etc.)
    6. Submit the request

**For Contractors and Team Members:**

    1. Visit the Shopify Collaborator Portal
    2. Enter the store URL
    3. Enter the collaborator code
    4. Select required permissions
    5. Wait for your approval

---

## Step 5: Review and Approve Access Requests

Once someone requests access using your code, you’ll receive a notification.

**To approve:**

    1. Go to Settings > Users and permissions
    2. Check the “Pending access requests” section
    3. Review the request
    4. Click “Approve” to grant access or “Reject” to decline

---
## Step 6: Manage Permissions

One of the biggest advantages is permission control. Only grant the minimum permissions needed:

**Available permissions include:**

    1. Theme development
    2. Online store
    3. Blog
    4. Product management
    5. Order management
    6. Customer management
    7. Marketing and analytics

---

## Step 7: Remove Access When Finished

Revoking access is simple and instant:

    1. Go to Settings > Users and permissions
    2. Find the collaborator in the list.
    3. Click the three-dot menu next to their name
    4. Select “Remove access”
    5. Confirm the removal
    6. Their access is revoked immediately.

## Common Questions

Q: Can I use the same code for multiple people? 

A: Yes! One code can be used by multiple developers.

Q: Do collaborators count toward my staff account limit? 

A: No. Collaborators don’t count toward your staff member limit.

Q: Is it safe to share my collaborator code? 

A: Relatively safe, but only share with trusted people.

Q: Can collaborators access my financial data? 

A: Only if you grant them those specific permissions.

Q: How many collaborators can I add? 

A: Unlimited! There’s no cap.

## Use Cases

- Hiring a Theme Developer: Grant theme development access only
- Adding a Content Manager: Give blog and product description access
- Virtual Assistant: Order management and customer service access
- Marketing Agency: Limited access to analytics and marketing tools
- Temporary Contractor: Full temporary access that you revoke when done

---

## Security Best Practices

*Do:*

- Only share codes with people you trust
- Regularly review who has access
- Grant minimum necessary permissions
- Remove access immediately when someone leaves
- Enable two-factor authentication on your main account

**Don’t:**

- Share your collaborator code publicly
- Grant permissions you don’t understand
- Leave old collaborators with access after projects end
- Share with unknown third parties

---


## Troubleshooting

Issue: Collaborator code not appearing Solution: Make sure you’re logged in as the store owner.

Issue: Developer can’t find “Request Access” Solution: Direct them to the Shopify Collaborator Portal instead.

Issue: Collaborator lost access unexpectedly Solution: Check if access was accidentally revoked. Re-invite them using the same code.
