## Beyond Work

Most people know me as a Shopify developer. Some know me as an Accounting & Finance student. Both are 
true, but neither tells the whole story., here's what actually makes up my days.

**Chess** is one of my quiet obsessions. I love chess. Not because of winning, but because every move has 
consequences. It teaches patience in a world that rewards speed. It teaches you that the best decision 
isn't always the obvious one. More often than not, the problems I solve for clients feel surprisingly 
similar: understanding the position, seeing what's hidden, and thinking a few moves ahead.

**I write.** Not for an audience, mostly for myself. Not for followers or algorithms. Just for clarity.
Sometimes it's a note on my phone. Sometimes it's a thought that refuses to leave. Sometimes it's a few
lines inspired by Urdu poetry. Writing has a way of slowing life down long enough to understand it. In a
world where everyone is trying to be heard, I've found value in simply paying attention.

**Islamic history** genuinely fascinates me not just the events, but the ideas, the 
civilizations, the scholars, the turning points. The golden age of the Islamic world, the 
House of Wisdom in Baghdad, the way knowledge traveled across continents through manuscripts 
and trade routes. It's the kind of history that makes you think differently about the present.

**Design matters to me.** I notice when something is visually intentional good typography, 
thoughtful layout, the feeling a well-built page gives. This is probably why I enjoy the 
front-end side of Shopify work more than most — I'm not just making things functional, 
I want them to feel right.

And then there's the strange, honest reality of being both a Shopify developer and an 
Accounting & Finance student at the same time two completely different disciplines that 
somehow make sense together when you're running a freelance agency and managing your own 
books. Multan raised me practical.
